Cambridge Primary (Level 4-6)

These levels lay a strong foundation in English, Afrikaans, Mathematics and Science with the comprehensive Cambridge curriculum designed to foster critical thinking and creativity.

Cambridge Lower Secondary (Level 7-9)

The Lower Secondary Level is particularly beneficial in preparing the student for the next phase of academic pursuits. Subjects like English, Afrikaans, Mathematics and Science prepare the students to face the challenges of higher education.

At this stage of academic development, students face significant decisions that can shape their educational and career paths.

Their academic progress at Level 9 will be central to their subject choices for further studies.

ACADEMIC OPTION 1: Cambridge IGCSE, AS and A-Level

A minimum grade of 60% in each subject is necessary for students wishing to pursue the demanding IGCSE, AS, and A-level Cambridge route. A consistent record of high marks and a matric exemption grants admission to most national and international universities.

Students who want to pursue the challenging IGCSE, AS, and A-level Cambridge route must have a minimum mark of 60% in each subject. Most national and foreign colleges admit students with a matric exemption and a persistent record of high grades.

ACADEMIC OPTION 2: Alternative Career paths

An interest in obtaining a matric certificate, vocational training, or a limited number of academic careers are alternatives.

Matriculation Certificate: It typically represents the successful completion of secondary education and may open doors to university or college programmes.

Vocational Training: Vocational training is a practical and hands-on approach to learning specific skills for a particular trade or profession.

For this choice, a pre-GED and a *GED are accepted at UNISA, Varsity College and University of the People and the University of Johannesburg.
