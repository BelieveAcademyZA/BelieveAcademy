Academic Choices

Primary School

Cambridge Primary (Level 4-6) — Classes presented in English

These levels lay a strong foundation in English (First Language), Afrikaans (Second Language), Mathematics and Science with the comprehensive Cambridge curriculum designed to foster critical thinking and creativity.

OR

CAPS (Grade 4-6) – Classes presented in either Afrikaans or English

The curriculum includes English (Home Language or Additional Language), Afrikaans (Huistaal of Addisionele Taal), Life Skills, Mathematics, Natural Science and Technology and Social Sciences.

Middle School

Cambridge Lower Secondary (Level 7-9) – Classes presented in English

The Lower Secondary Level is particularly beneficial in preparing the student for the next phase of academic pursuits. Subjects like English, Afrikaans, Mathematics and Science prepare the students to face the challenges of higher education.

OR

CAPS (Grade 7-9) – Classes presented in either Afrikaans or English

The curriculum includes English (Home Language or Additional Language), Afrikaans (Huistaal of Addisionele Taal), Creative Arts, Economic Management Sciences, Life Orientation, Mathematics, Natural Sciences, Social Sciences and Technology.

Students face significant decisions that can shape their educational and career paths at this stage of academic development. Their academic progress at Level 9 will be central to their subject choices for further studies.

Secondary School

Cambridge System (Level 10-13) – Cambridge IGCSE, AS and A-Level (presented in English)

A minimum grade of 60% in each Level/Grade 9 subject is necessary for students wishing to pursue the demanding IGCSE, AS, and A-level Cambridge route. A consistent record of high marks and a matric exemption grants admission to most national and international universities.

This system is subdivided into two parts:

Part 1: IGCSE (International General Certificate in Secondary Education)

This 2-year programme corresponds academically with South Africa’s grades 10 and 11. Support is presented for English First Language, Afrikaans Second Language, Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Business Studies and Religious Studies. IGCSE is a demanding study that requires 60% per subject to pass. Some universities and colleges accept the IGCSE certificate as sufficient for further studies.

Part 2: AS & A-Levels (Advanced Subsidiary & Advanced levels)

Each level is described as a 1-year course and the A-level (equivalent to Grade 13) follows the AS-level (equivalent to Grade 12). However, students may directly enroll for the A-level after passing the IGCSE (60%). The Believe Academy assists in all subjects listed in the IGCSE section.

Subject combinations and matric exemption are discussed separately.

OR

CAPS (FET Phase; Grade 10-12) — Impaq (presented in Afrikaans or English)

Four subjects are compulsory: Afrikaans (Huistaal of Eerste Addisionele Taal), English (Home Language or First Additional Language), Mathematics or Mathematical Literacy and Life Orientation.

In addition, three subjects must be selected as electives. The Believe Academy can assist students with Accounting, Business Studies, Physical Sciences, Economics and Life Sciences.

OR

GED (General Educational Development) (Grade 10-12)

Get ready for success in the GED examination with our tailored program. That will ensure you have the knowledge and skills to ace the test and pursue your academic goals. Subjects are English, Mathematics, Science and Social Studies. Psychology and Therapy lectures are added for Neuro-Diversity students.
